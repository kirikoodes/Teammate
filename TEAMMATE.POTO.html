How it works

Listens to the microphone continuously and records sounds into a 48-slot corpus. Once 4 slots are filled, it starts improvising — responding to what it hears with its own phrases. Rhy (K2 on p.4) sets the probability that a phrase locks onto a BPM grid (1/8 or 1/16 note) — 0 / 15 / 30 / 50%.

POtO — real-time granular synthesis on the last 4 seconds of audio. Grain size and spread on p.7.

8OS — 20s looper. K3 → REC records. K3 → TRANS plays back as random grains.

MGEN — 16-channel MIDI sequencer. Enable channels on p.14, pick styles, start on p.13. Breaks (p.15) apply to active channels for one cycle. Evo (K2 on p.14) sets a per-step mutation rate — notes drift to adjacent scale degrees, gates and velocities shift gradually each cycle. K2 on p.13 changes theme live while running.

14 styles: TECHNO · DnB · JUNGLE · AMAPIANO · 2STEP · BRKN · DUMB · TRAP · DRIL · CLUB · KPOP · ORNTL · RAVE · TRNCE.

MIDI routing — each stream (IMPRO / POtO / 8OS / MGEN) can be routed to up to 4 MIDI devices on pages 9–12. Audio→MIDI (p.16) converts live audio pitch to polyphonic MIDI notes in real time.

SPAT (p.17) — animates each voice in the stereo field using one of 6 physics models: NEBULA (Brownian), ORBIT (Keplerian), PULSAR (tanh arc sweep), QUANTUM (stable + RMS tunneling), STRANGE (Lorenz attractor), ENTANGLE (Lead↔Attracted mirrored). E2 = mass (amplitude), E3 = tempo (speed). When POtO SMRT is active, mass and tempo are auto-modulated by the detected technique. Markers on the L–C–R line: * IMPRO · O LEAD · o ATTRACTED · .
